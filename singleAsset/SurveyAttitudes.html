Personal Carbon Trading (PCT) is a policy designed to reduce a country's carbon dioxide ('carbon') emissions by capping total individual emissions. In such a scheme, every person receives an equal number of carbon credits each year (their allowance) that can be used to purchase emissions-producing goods and services such as petrol, electricity, heating oil, or flights.

People who want to consume more emissions-producing goods than their allowance permits can buy extra credits from those with a smaller environmental footprint. High emitters pay for additional carbon credits, while low emitters can benefit financially by selling any spare credits. Overall, total individual emissions stay the same, and in a real PCT scheme, the number of carbon credits allocated to each person would typically be reduced gradually over time to progressively lower total emissions.
