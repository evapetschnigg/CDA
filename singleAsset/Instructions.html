Aim of the study

The purpose of the study is to investigate trading behavior in an experimental asset market.

Structure of the study

The study will proceed as follows:

  1. Comprehension check: A short quiz to make sure the rules are understood.
  2. Trial round: One practice trading period.
  3. Main part: 8 trading rounds, each lasting 2.5 minutes.
  4. Final part: A brief survey.

Overall, the study will take about 30 minutes to complete.

The Market Place

You will interact with other participants through a market screen (see screenshot below). The layout is the same in all rounds, including the trial.

  • The group of participants you interact with remains the same throughout the study.
  • At the start of each round, you will receive a new endowment of money and assets. Endowments do not carry over between rounds.
  • Your goal in each round is to maximize your Total Score by trading assets with other participants and buying goods.

Endowment

Before each trading round begins, your assigned endowment of money and assets will be displayed. You will always start the round without any goods. Once the round starts, your holdings are shown in the white box on the market screen.

The Goods Market

  • You can buy two types of goods: Good A and Good B.
  • Goods have fixed costs in money and assets and give you satisfaction points.
  • When you buy a good, the required money and assets are deducted, and the satisfaction points are added to your Total Score.
  • You may buy as many goods as you want, as long as you have enough money and assets.
  • Goods can only be bought, not sold.

The Asset Market

  • The asset market is a double auction market where you trade assets with other participants.
  • In the top half of the asset market you may post:
    • Bids (buy offers): enter the price at which you want to buy an asset.
    • Asks (sell offers): enter the price at which you want to sell an asset.
  • All bids and asks are displayed in the lower half of the market.
  • To accept another participant’s bid or ask, select it and click Sell or Buy. Your asset and money holdings will be updated automatically.
  • Your own posted offers appear in red. You cannot accept your own offers, but you may cancel them at any time by selecting them and clicking Cancel.
  • The trade history graph shows the timing and prices of asset trades made between participants in your group.

Alerts

The Latest Alert box at the bottom of the market screen informs you if you attempt an action that is not allowed. Examples include:

  • Buying or selling at a negative price.
  • Cancelling another participant’s bid or ask.
  • Accepting your own bid or ask.
  • Buying more than you can afford.
  • Trying to accept a worse offer when a better one is available.

If you attempt such an action, a message will appear in the Latest Alert box to inform you why the action is not allowed.

Total Score

Your goal is to maximize your Total Score, as this will affect your payout.

Total Score = Money holding + Satisfaction points from goods

Example:
Imagine you start a round with 10 assets, 100 monetary units (mu) and 0 goods. This gives you an initial Total Score of 100.

You can increase your Score by buying goods or selling assets.

  • If you buy Good A (cost: 5 mu and 1 asset, value: 12 satisfaction points),
    your new Score is: 100 – 5 mu + 12 points = 107.
  • If you to sell one asset for 10 mu,
    your new Score is: 100 + 10 mu = 110.

Important: Assets themselves do not count toward your Score. However, they are valuable as you may trade them for a profit or use them to buy goods. Unused assets expire at the end of each round.

Your Payout

Your payout for this study consists of two parts:

  1. A base payout of €15.
  2. A performance-based payout that depends on your Score Change in a randomly selected round.

The Score Change is defined as:

Score Change (%) = (Final Score − Initial Score) / Initial Score

The Score Change in the randomly selected round i is then multiplied by 90 and added to your base payout of €15. Your payout cannot be less than €4, therefore the calculation is:

Payout = max(€4; €15 + Score Change in round i × 90)

Example:
Initial Score in round i = 100, Final Score in round i = 120
→ Score Change = 20%.
Payout = €15 + (20% × 90) = €15 + €18 = €33.

The trial round is not payout-relevant. You will only receive your payout if you complete the full study, including the survey, in one sitting. Breaks in between are not possible.
