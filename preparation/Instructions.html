Aim of the Study

The study investigates trading behavior in an experimental {% if framing == 'environmental' or framing == 'destruction' %}personal carbon market{% else %}asset market{% endif %}.

Structure

The study consists of:

  • Comprehension check
  • Trial round (1 practice trading period)
  • Main part (6 trading rounds of 80 seconds each)
  • Final survey

Total time: about 25 minutes.

The Marketplace

You will interact with 5 other participants through a market interface (see screenshot below).

The layout is the same in all rounds, including the trial.

  • You stay with the same group throughout the study.
  • At the start of each round, you receive a new endowment of money and {% if framing == 'environmental' or framing == 'destruction' %}carbon credits{% else %}assets{% endif %}.
    • {% if framing == 'environmental' or framing == 'destruction' %}
  • Carbon credits represent the right to emit CO₂ and are needed (together with money) to buy goods.
    • {% endif %}
  • Your goal is to maximize your Total Score by trading {% if framing == 'environmental' or framing == 'destruction' %}carbon credits{% else %}assets{% endif %} and buying goods.

Endowment

Before each round begins, you will see your money and {% if framing == 'environmental' or framing == 'destruction' %}carbon credit{% else %}asset{% endif %} endowment.

  • All participants receive the same number of {% if framing == 'environmental' or framing == 'destruction' %}carbon credits{% else %}assets{% endif %} .
  • {% if endowment_type == 'homogeneous' %}Money endowments are the same for everyone.{% else %}Money endowments differ and are assigned randomly.{% endif %}
  • Everyone starts the round with zero goods.
  • Your current holdings appear in the white box on the market screen.
  • Holdings do not carry over between rounds.

Goods Market

In the goods market you can buy two types of goods: Good A and Good B.

  • Goods cost money and {% if framing == 'environmental' or framing == 'destruction' %}carbon credits{% else %}assets{% endif %}.
  • Buying a good gives you satisfaction points, which increase your Total Score.
  • Prices (in money and {% if framing == 'environmental' or framing == 'destruction' %}carbon credits{% else %}assets{% endif %}) are the same for everyone, but satisfaction points differ across participants. You see your own points on-screen.
  • You may buy any number of goods as long as you can afford them.
  • Goods cannot be sold.

{% if framing == 'environmental' or framing == 'destruction' %}Carbon{% else %}Asset{% endif %} Market

In the {% if framing == 'environmental' or framing == 'destruction' %}carbon market{% else %}asset market{% endif %} you may buy {% if framing == 'environmental' or framing == 'destruction' %}carbon credits{% else %}assets{% endif %} from or sell them to other participants.

Placing offers

  • Bids (to buy): enter the price you are willing to pay for one {% if framing == 'environmental' or framing == 'destruction' %}credit{% else %}asset{% endif %}.
  • Asks (to sell): enter the price you want to receive.

Your active offers appear in red and can be cancelled at any time.
You cannot accept your own offers.
When another participants accepts your offer, your holdings update automatically.

Accepting offers

All active bids and asks appear in the lower half of the market.
Select an offer and click Buy or Sell to trade.
Your holdings update automatically.

Alerts

If you attempt an action that is not allowed—such as trying to cancel another participant’s bid—the yellow Latest Alert box at the bottom of the screen will explain why.
This system ensures the market works properly and prevents invalid or non-beneficial trades.

Total Score

Your goal is to maximize your Total Score as this will affect your payout.

Total Score = Money holding + Satisfaction points from goods

Example:
Imagine you start a round with 10 {% if framing == 'environmental' or framing == 'destruction' %}carbon credits{% else %}assets{% endif %}, 100 monetary units (mu) and 0 goods.
This gives you an initial Total Score of 100.
You can increase your Total Score by buying goods or selling {% if framing == 'environmental' or framing == 'destruction' %}carbon credits{% else %}assets{% endif %}:

  • If you buy Good A (price: 5 mu and 1 {% if framing == 'environmental' or framing == 'destruction' %}carbon credit{% else %}asset{% endif %}, value: 12 satisfaction points),
    your new Score is: 100 – 5 mu + 12 points = 107.
  • If you to sell one {% if framing == 'environmental' or framing == 'destruction' %}carbon credit{% else %}asset{% endif %} for 10 mu,
    your new Score is: 100 + 10 mu = 110.

{% if framing == 'environmental' or framing == 'destruction' %}Carbon credits{% else %}Assets{% endif %} do not directly count toward your Score.
However, they are valuable because you can trade them for profit or use them to buy goods.
{% if framing != 'destruction' %} Unused {% if framing == 'environmental' %}carbon credits{% else %}assets{% endif %} expire at the end of each round. {% endif %}

    {% if framing == 'destruction' %}
  • Unused carbon credits do not carry over to the next round and reduce your group's total emissions.
  • To make this effect tangible, in one randomly selected round, the experimenter will purchase CO₂ compensation certificates from the non-profit organization ForTomorrow for each unused carbon credit in the experiment.
  • Each certificate removes 1 kg of CO₂ from the atmosphere through reforestation projects in Germany — about as much as is emitted during a 7 km car drive.
  • Your decisions in this experiment therefore have a real and measurable impact on carbon emissions.

Payout

Your payout has two parts:

  • Base payment: £3.75
  • Bonus payment: £1.90 for the participant with the highest Score Change in a randomly selected round.
Score Change (%) = (Final Score − Initial Score) / Initial Score

The player with the highest Score Change in the randomly selected round will receive the bonus payment of £1.90 on top of the base payment of £3.75, for a total payout of £5.65. The other players will receive only the base payment of £3.75.

  • If there is a tie, the winner is chosen randomly among the top scorers.
  • The trial round is not payout-relevant.
  • You must complete the entire study (including the survey) in one sitting to receive payment.
  • You may have to wait a bit for other participants to form a group. If you cannot be matched with enough other participants to form a group, you will end the study early and receive the base payment as compensation.
