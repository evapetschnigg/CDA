This study is carried out under the supervision of the Professorship of Economics at the Technical University of Munich, Campus Straubing (TUMCS). The professorship conducts research on consumer and market decisions at the interface between science and practice and examines how consumer and business decisions change in markets.

{% if framing == 'baseline' %} This study investigates trading behavior in an experimental asset market. {% else %} This study investigates trading behavior in an experimental personal carbon market. {% endif %}

Participation in the study is voluntary. All tasks in the study are harmless and do not convey any physical or psychological risks. Completing the study will take about 25 minutes. The study consists of two parts. In the main part, participants trade assets with each other over several rounds. The second part is a brief survey.

Scope of data processing

The information you provide during the study will be processed by the TUMCS Professorship of Economics exclusively for the purpose of evaluating the results of the study, and for further academic research purposes. The legal basis for the processing of your data is your consent (Art. 6 (1) a) GDPR). Personal data collected in the course of the study will not be passed on to other third parties unless we are explicitly obliged to do so by law. Data processing takes place exclusively within the EU. Personal data is deleted once the purpose for which it was collected no longer applies and in accordance with the statutory deletion periods.

Your rights

As the data subject you have the following rights if we process your personal data:

  • You have the right to access information on the data stored pertaining to your person (Art. 15 GDPR).
  • Should incorrect personal data be processed, you have the right to rectification (Art. 16 GDPR).
  • If the legal requirements are met, you have the right to request the erasure or restriction of processing (Art. 17 and 18 GDPR).
  • If you have agreed to the processing of your data or if a contract for data processing has been concluded and the data processing is carried out by automated means, you may have the right to data portability (Art. 20 GDPR).
  • If you have agreed to the processing and the processing is based on this consent, you have the right to object to the future processing of your data at any time. The withdrawal of consent shall not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal (Art. 21 GDPR) .
  • Restrictions and modifications of the aforementioned rights may ensue from Art. 9 and 10 BayDSG and Art. 20 BayDSG.
  • You have the right to object to the processing of your data at any time for reasons arising from your particular situation, if the processing is carried out exclusively on the basis of Article 6 paragraph 1 letter e or f GDPR (Article 21 paragraph 1 sentence 1 GDPR).
